The Problem
Logistics moves economies. Yet most logistics decisions across Africa are still made without complete information.

Thousands of shipments move every day between ports, warehouses, factories, and customers. Each movement creates valuable data — but that information is scattered across spreadsheets, WhatsApp messages, paper documents, and disconnected systems.

❌ Today's reality
📋Logistics companies manage fleets with spreadsheets and phone calls
🏦Banks finance cargo without operational visibility into risk
⚠️Delays are discovered only when they become crises
📊Insurers price cargo risk with incomplete route data
🏛️Governments lack a live view of national logistics health
🔗Every operator works in isolation from the network
→ The consequences
💸Higher operational costs across every corridor
⏱️Delayed deliveries that compound into supply chain failures
❌Reliable transport businesses can't access trade finance
📈Insurance premiums based on guesswork, not data
🌍Trade growth constrained by invisible infrastructure gaps
🤝Development finance misallocated without corridor intelligence

The challenge is not the absence of data. The challenge is transforming that data into intelligence people can trust and act on.
